# **Personal Growth & Alignment Life OS**

### **Overview**

A flexible, visual system for managing **ongoing personal growth**, **habits**, and **self-improvement**—not just tasks.
Designed to connect long-term vision with short-term reflection through cyclical review and continuous alignment.

---

## **1. System Structure**

### **A. Life Domains**

1. **Family & Relationships** – Marriage, parenting, emotional connection
2. **Healthy Aging & Longevity** – Long-term vitality, preventive care, research
3. **Wellness** – Physical and mental health, mindfulness, daily energy
4. **Life Admin** – Finances, household, errands, maintenance

---

### **B. Workflow Stages**

| **Column**  | **Purpose**                                                                        | **Examples / Notes**                                                                                           |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0. Guiding Principles / Identity**  | Your compass — static core statements describing who you are becoming. | “I am a patient and present father.”“I make disciplined choices for long-term health.” |
| **1. Long-Term Outcomes (1–5 Years)**  | Tangible indicators that you’re living your principles.                | “My sons confide in me regularly.”“My key health metrics are optimal for my age.”      |
| **2. Quarterly Initiatives**  | 90-day focus areas translating outcomes into concrete processes.        | “Weekly one-on-one time with each son.”“Complete longevity consultation.”              |
| **3. Weekly Review Prompts (Backlog)**  | Reflection cards for weekly assessment.                                 | “Review: Family Presence.”“Review: Wellness Metrics.”                                  |
| **4. In Progress**  *(WIP limit: 1–2)* | The active reflections you’re working on this week.                    | Pull 1–2 review prompts at a time.                                                        |
| **5. Done & Learnings**  | Completed reflections with captured insights.                           | Add a short note on what you learned. Archive after review.                                |

---

## **2. Review Cadence**

### **A. Daily Touchpoint (5 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  Ground yourself in intention.
- **Action:**

  - Glance at *Guiding Principles*.
  - Choose one to live intentionally today.
  - No card movement or tracking.

---

### **B. Weekly Review (30–45 minutes)**

- **Purpose:**  Evaluate progress and extract insights.
- **Process:**

  1. **Celebrate & Clear:**  Review “Done & Learnings.” Note insights. Archive.
  2. **Pull & Reflect:**  Move 1–2 prompts from Backlog → In Progress.
  3. **Deep Dive:**  Journal or think through guided questions:

      - Scale (1–10): How aligned was I this week?
      - What worked? What felt meaningful?
      - What got in the way?
      - What’s one small shift for next week?
  4. **Capture Insight:**  Add 1 takeaway or micro-goal. Move card → Done & Learnings.

---

### **C. Quarterly / Annual Retreat (2–3 hours)**

- **Purpose:**  Recalibrate long-term direction.
- **Actions:**

  - **Reflect:**  Read past “Done & Learnings.” Identify patterns and growth themes.
  - **Assess:**  Review *Long-Term Outcomes*. Adjust or reaffirm.
  - **Plan:**  Define next *Quarterly Initiatives* (2–3 max).
  - **Create:**  Generate new *Weekly Review Prompts* for each initiative.
  - **Refine:**  Update *Guiding Principles* if needed.

---

## **3. Implementation Tips**

- **Tools:**

  - *Digital:*  Notion or Obsidian for flexible linking between reflections and goals.
  - *Physical:*  Whiteboard + sticky notes for tactile engagement.
- **Keep it Lean:**  Start with only a few Guiding Principles and one life domain.
- **Evolve Naturally:**  Add columns, prompts, or templates as reflection deepens.
- **WIP Discipline:**  Limit to 1–2 active reflections; depth over volume.

---

## **4. Core Mindset**

This Life OS is about more than “getting things done.”
It’s about **becoming the kind of person who lives deliberately** — seeing your growth as a continuous flow of awareness, insight, and refinement.
