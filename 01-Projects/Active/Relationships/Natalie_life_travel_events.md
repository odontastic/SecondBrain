### Mediterranean Diet Principles

**Core Components:**
- **Fresh fruits and vegetables:** At least one serving of veg per meal
- **Seafood:** Emphasize fish for heart-healthy omega-3s; also poultry
- **Healthy fats and oils:** Extra-virgin olive oil (hero ingredient), walnut oil, pecan oil
- **Legumes:** Lentils, beans, chickpeas, peas (multiple times per week)
- **Nuts, seeds, and grains:** Whole-grain cereals, as snacks, on salads
- **Fermented dairy:** Yogurt and goat cheese (once daily) for gut health

**Benefits:**
- Reduces inflammation by cutting high-sugar foods, highly processed foods, red meats, and alcohol
- Increases inflammation-reducing foods: plants, omega-3 fatty acids, monounsaturated fats

**Source:** https://www.gq.com/story/what-is-mediterranean-diet

### "Outlive: The Science & Art of Longevity" by Peter Attia, M.D.

**Key Concept:** Not about lifespan but HEALTHSPAN - how to live longer and live better in later years (the "Marginal Decade")

**The 4 Horsemen (Diseases Most People Die From):**
1. Heart disease
2. Cancer
3. Neurodegenerative diseases (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's)
4. Metabolic dysfunction (diabetes)

**THE KEY:** PREVENTION earlier in life

**5 Tools for Prevention:**

#### I. EXERCISE: Aerobic, Strength, and Stability

**Aerobic Conditioning:**
1. **Zone 2 Endurance Training:** Four 45-minute sessions per week at steady pace where you can still talk (treadmill or stationary bike)
2. **VO2 Max:** Hard push/run for 4 minutes, then jog 4 minutes easy where heart rate drops below 100/min. Repeat 4-6 times and cool down

**Strength Training:**
- Protein intake
- Load-bearing activities
- Hormone replacement therapy (HRT)
- Drugs to increase bone density as indicated
- Focus on: Grip strength, ability to carry and put down heavy items (concentric/eccentric loading), pulling motions, hip-hinging movements (strengthen legs, glutes, lower back)

**Stability Training:**
- Strong, fluid, flexible, agile
- Dynamic Neuromuscular Stabilization (DNS)
- Postural Restoration Institute (PRI)
- Focus on: breathing, feet firmly planted, balance, spine protection
- Goal: Neuromuscular control

#### II. NUTRITION

**Approach:** Biochemistry - scientifically rigorous, highly personalized, driven by feedback and data

**Focus Areas:**
- Nourishment
- Muscles
- Metabolic health

**Goal:** Reduce energy intake while adding lean mass (consume fewer calories, increase protein intake, pair with proper exercise)

**3 Methods:**
1. Caloric restriction (CR)
2. Dietary restriction
3. Time restriction (intermittent fasting)

**Key Findings from Long-Term Monkey Research:**
1. Avoiding diabetes and related metabolic dysfunction important to longevity
2. Strong link between calories and cancer
3. Quality of food as important as quantity
4. If diet already high quality and metabolically healthy, only slight degree of caloric restriction can still be beneficial

**Monitoring Tools:**
- Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)
- Biomarkers: lean mass, lipids, uric acid, insulin, liver enzymes, ApoB and Lp(a) levels
- DEXA scan: measure bone density and muscle mass

**Protein:** VERY IMPORTANT
- Need 4 servings daily: protein shake, protein snack, 2 protein meals

**Fats:**
- **Saturated and polyunsaturated:** Good
- **PUFA (Polyunsaturated):** Omega-6 (corn, soybean) or omega-3s (marine like fish with EPA/DHA; non-marine like nuts and flaxseed)
- **MUFA (Monounsaturated):** Probably best (olive oil)

**Recommended Diet:**
- Olive oil
- Avocado
- Nuts
- Salmon
- Anchovies

**Fasting Benefits:**
- Forces body to mobilize fat stores
- Turns down mTOR (pro-growth and pro-aging pathway)

#### III. SLEEP

**Requirement:** At least 7.5 hours each night

**Sleep Deprivation Consequences:**
- Insulin resistance
- Increased risk of cardiovascular disease
- Impaired cognitive health (risk of Alzheimer's and dementia)
- High stress levels activate sympathetic response and metabolic dysfunction

**Sleep Stages:**
- **REM Sleep:** Dream stage; important for creativity, problem-solving, procedural memory, processing emotional memories, maintaining emotional awareness
- **Deep NREM Sleep:** Long-term memory consolidation in hippocampus; brain cleanup (clearing out amyloid beta and tau - two proteins found in Alzheimer's patients)

**Sleep Medications:**
- Ambien or benzodiazepines induce unconsciousness without improving sleep quality
- Exception: Trazodone at low dose can improve sleep architecture (also supplement ashwagandha)

**Assessment Tools:**
- Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (best validated)
- Epworth Sleepiness Scale
- Insomnia Severity Index
- STOP-BANG questionnaire (rule out sleep apnea)
- Sleep study

**Tips for Better Sleep:**
- Room darkness
- Limit LED light exposure 2 hours before bed
- Limit cellphone/computer use
- Cool bedroom (65Â°F)
- Warm bath before bed
- No alcohol or caffeine
- Exercise during the day
- Avoid stressful thoughts
- Meditation
- Avoid foods within 3 hours of bedtime
- **Goal:** Increase "sleep pressure"

## Book Summary: "How Will You Measure Your Life?" by Clayton Christensen

Success in life is not solely about achieving professional goals or accumulating wealth. It's about finding personal happiness, maintaining good relationships, and living with integrity. It's important to have a clear sense of purpose and to align your actions with your values.

Life is a delicate balance of personal and professional commitments. It's crucial to invest time and energy in your relationships and personal growth, just as you would in your career. The most fulfilling lives are those filled with meaningful relationships and activities, not just professional achievements.

It's also essential to maintain your integrity and stay true to your moral compass. Compromising your values for short-term gains can lead to long-term dissatisfaction and regret.

Remember, your life's measure is not in material possessions, but in the lives you've touched, the love you've shared, and the values you've upheld.

In the end, it's not about how much you've achieved, but who you've become as a person. So, strive to live a life that you can be proud of, a life that truly reflects who you are and what you believe in.
